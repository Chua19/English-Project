Injuries

Because you practice for hours on end, you stay competitive with your peers. However, you begin to feel sore after practicing, and you start having some eye fatigue. You're pretty sure it's nothing, and don't have it checked by a professional. There is also a competition that is coming up that you really want to prepare for.

You can either: choose to continue to practice and put more strain on your body or stop to recover but lose a lot of practice time for the event.

  • Stop and Rest
  • Train for Competition
×

Because you decided to continue practicing, you develop injuries, which is fairly common for college esports athletes. After interviewing 65 college esports athletes, the most reported complaint given was eye fatigue, followed by neck and back pain, wrist pain, and hand pain (DiFrancisco-Donoghue, 3). Because of these injuries you are unable to continue playing.
