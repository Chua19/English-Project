Background

You're an 18 year old that just graduated high school. You've been playing video games for a couple of years now, and have become pretty good. You're one of the top 300 players in the world, and you've slowly been climbing the leaderboards.

You have a choice to make though. You've got offers from multiple colleges to come and compete for their team. You've also got a few offers from professional teams. Most colleges that accepted you have offered you full scholarships to their school while the teams that you've talked to are willing to give you contracts that last a year and pay about the same amount as the scholarships.

  • Go to a Professional Team

You play with the team for a couple of months, but then you get dropped from the team. What happened? Because you decided to skip college esports altogether, you lost some valuable skills. In a study conducted by Claire Schaeperkoetter from the University of Kansas, all 33 college esports athletes that were interviewed stated that they had formed a strong bond with their teammates (15). This level of communication and teamwork is extremely important when competing, as there are many team videogames that require all team members to be in sync. The college esports programs give players the experience that cannot be replicated anywhere else, which extremely important for players looking to go professional.
